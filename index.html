At Risk Species in Australia 2023


As a general rule there are more at risk species in the more cosmopolitan areas of Australia's South east. This could be a consequence of these areas being the most surveyed area in the country. The inland slopes region in particular has the largest number of at risk species 851. Also if interest is the perth region, which has an at risk count of 509. This could consider systematic issues with Perth's environmental management, or just a statistical coincidence.


The International Union for the Conservation of Nature (IUCN) classifies species which are 'at risk' from 'least concern' to 'critically endangered'. The vast majority of catalogued at risk species in Australia are birds (Class Aves). Victoria had the most at risk species, with over 20,000 being identified in 2023. The ACT had by far the least at risk species, which is to be expected given it's relatively small geographic entity.
